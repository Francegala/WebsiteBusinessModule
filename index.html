When you know how to bring your project to people who need it, you will need to create a product that is worth protecting assigning it elements that help your customers recognise you, such as a logo, a name, a colour or a motto. 

how to bring your creation on the
market?

To better know what aspects you must highlight more of your idea, you need to ask your consumers.

You can choose between two types of market research:
Primary Research) you can create surveys or interview your customers directly
Secondary Research) you can search for data online or using academic journals and newspapers to read market reports.

It would help if you also researched for competitors and for the right price to lead the market

Data from your research will help you understand what you need to change from your original idea and if the market is ready for your product.

My motivations

Last year I had the chance to have my first taste of creating a startup.
I started a project with some friends aiming to create an app to connect people during the quarantine.
I had no previous business knowledge, so I mainly rely on searching on Google, but it's been very time-consuming.
That's why I wanted to learn more on business and marketing!
In the future I'll know the right steps for doing a market research,
And all the resoures I need to evaluate my idea.

Go to the project!
Survey from my group project

In my group project, we knew there was an issue related to mental health at university due to COVID 19.
From research, many studies were discussing this topic highliting how the COVID impacted students' achievements.
Therefore, our survey were asking questions around deadlines and university work.
Since I read the book "The mom test", a book helping to understand how to phrase questions,
I knew that to know more about this I had not to look for a confirm but to know about their mental health in general.
In this way I discovered that people were not only looking for an help for assessment and work
but they needed something to deal with stress and to organise their day better
considering their hobbies and activities outside the university.
